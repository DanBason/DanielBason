Case Study





Most Effective Method

A Digital Detox is an excellent way to take a step back and access that role that technology and social media play in your life

The Cleveland Clinic writes "Doing a digital detox is a great way to find out if technology is holding you back from living your best life. The results of unplugging can be far reaching, from being more productive at work to deepening your relationships with family and friends."



The most effective way to address the root causes of the issue is to raise awareness and understanding about the nature and consequences of social media.


Educating people about the effects of social media can help bring awareness to the seriously negative conseqeuences.

The internet has a variety of sources that talk about the issue. Whether you're more interested in articles, books, or videos, there's thousands of resources to learn about experiences that other people have had.




Use of Technology

Examples of apps that relate to limiting internet/social media usage.

SelfControl is an open source application for mac that allows users to block their own access to websites. Freedom is an IOS app that also allows users to limit their screen time. FocusMate is an app that partners users with a human accountability partner.



There are an increasing number of apps and tools that can help people to separate social media from their use of the internet.

Implications

If more young people are educated on the negative effects of social media, they will be able to educated decisions about the trade-offs they are willing to make.

Social media is a relatively new concept, and as we are seeing currently, it's undergoing a bit of a revolution.

Research on social media and its effects are ongoing, and I believe that more in depth research on its long term effects will be presented in the near future. The whole twitter saga is also providing people with a moment of reflection; do they really need social media and what will life without twitter look like. The Facebook whistleblower also illustrated the critical issues with the way social media companies are willing to throw users under the bus. Moreover, Apple decision to provide users with more transparency has harbored social media companies' ability to advetise.
